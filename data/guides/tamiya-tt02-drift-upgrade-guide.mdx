# Tamiya TT02 Drift Upgrade Guide (Beginner to Advanced)

The Tamiya TT02 is one of the most popular entry-level RC chassis, but its stock configuration is designed for touring car racing, not drifting. While the TT02 offers excellent value and durability, its factory setup presents several limitations for drift enthusiasts. The plastic bushings, basic suspension geometry, and limited steering angle make it challenging to achieve consistent drift performance out of the box.

This guide provides a systematic approach to upgrading your TT02 for optimal drift performance, organized by priority and difficulty level.

## Upgrade Priority

When planning your TT02 drift build, follow this logical upgrade sequence:

1. **Essential Foundation**: Suspension and steering modifications
2. **Performance Enhancement**: Electronics and drivetrain upgrades  
3. **Fine-tuning**: Advanced chassis tuning and weight distribution
4. **Competition Ready**: Professional-level adjustments and custom modifications

## Chassis & Suspension

### Essential Upgrades
- **Ball Bearings**: Replace plastic bushings with full ball bearing sets for smoother rotation and reduced friction
- **Aluminum Oil Shocks**: Upgrade to adjustable oil-filled dampers for consistent damping and rebound control
- **Suspension Arms**: Consider adjustable aluminum arms for precise camber and toe angle tuning

### Advanced Modifications
- **Carbon Fiber Chassis**: Lightweight alternative for improved rigidity and weight reduction
- **Adjustable Motor Mount**: Allows for optimal weight distribution and motor positioning
- **Anti-roll Bars**: Install sway bars to control body roll during transitions

## Steering & Gyro

### Steering System
- **Steering Upgrade Kit**: Essential for increasing steering angle beyond stock limitations
- **Aluminum Steering Parts**: Replace plastic components for more precise steering response
- **Servo Saver Adjustment**: Tune or replace for direct steering feel

### Gyro Selection
- **Entry Level**: Basic gyro with adjustable gain for stability
- **Competition Grade**: Advanced gyro with multiple tuning parameters and quick response
- **Placement**: Mount gyro as close to chassis center as possible for optimal performance

## Tires & Wheels

### Tire Selection
- **Hard Compound**: Provides consistent slide characteristics on various surfaces
- **Medium Compound**: Balanced grip and slide for technical courses
- **Soft Compound**: Maximum grip for high-speed drift entries

### Wheel Considerations
- **Offset**: Choose appropriate wheel offset for proper track width and clearance
- **Weight**: Lighter wheels improve responsiveness, heavier wheels provide stability
- **Diameter**: Standard 26mm diameter works well for most TT02 applications

## Electronics Setup

### Motor and ESC
- **Brushless System**: 10.5T to 13.5T sensored brushless motors offer smooth power delivery
- **Programmable ESC**: Allows for precise throttle curve and brake force adjustment
- **Battery**: 2S LiPo batteries provide optimal power-to-weight ratio

### Radio System
- **2.4GHz Radio**: Digital proportional control with minimal interference
- **Programmable Features**: Endpoint adjustment, exponential curves, and dual-rate settings
- **Response Time**: Look for systems with fast response for precise control

## Beginner vs Advanced Upgrade Path

### Beginner Path (Essential Upgrades)
1. Ball bearings and aluminum oil shocks
2. Basic steering upgrade kit
3. Entry-level gyro and servo
4. Hard compound drift tires
5. 2S LiPo battery and basic charger

### Intermediate Path (Performance Focus)
1. Full aluminum steering system
2. Adjustable suspension arms and turnbuckles
3. Medium-grade brushless system
4. Competition-style gyro
5. Wheel and tire optimization

### Advanced Path (Competition Ready)
1. Carbon fiber chassis reinforcement
2. Custom suspension geometry modifications
3. High-performance brushless system with tuning software
4. Professional-grade gyro with multiple tuning modes
5. Weight distribution optimization and custom parts
