# TT-02 Drift Body Shell Guide

![JDM style drift body shell with aggressive styling and custom paint job](/static/images/articles/tt-02-drift-body-shell-guide-image-2.webp)

## Why Body Shells Matter for RC Drift Performance

Body shells are more than just cosmetic accessories - they play a crucial role in your TT-02's performance, protection, and overall driving experience. The right body shell can transform your drift car's handling characteristics while protecting your valuable components from impacts.

### Key Functions of Drift Body Shells:
- **Aerodynamic Performance** - Proper body design affects airflow and stability
- **Component Protection** - Shields electronics and mechanical parts from damage
- **Weight Distribution** - Affects balance and handling characteristics
- **Visual Identity** - Expresses your personal style and drift philosophy

## Types of Body Shells for TT-02 Drift Cars

### 1. Pre-Painted Body Shells
![Pre-painted body shell showing factory-applied graphics and colors](/static/images/articles/tt-02-drift-body-shell-guide-image-3.webp)

**Best For:** Beginners and those who want immediate results
- **Pros:** Ready to use, consistent quality, no painting skills required
- **Cons:** Limited customization, higher cost, less unique appearance
- **Popular Brands:** Tamiya, HPI, Yokomo, MST

### 2. Clear Lexan Body Shells (DIY Painting)
![Clear lexan body shell ready for custom painting and detailing](/static/images/articles/tt-02-drift-body-shell-guide-image-4.webp)

**Best For:** Intermediate to advanced users who want full customization
- **Pros:** Unlimited design possibilities, cost-effective, personal satisfaction
- **Cons:** Requires painting skills and equipment, time-consuming
- **Material:** 0.5mm to 1.0mm thick polycarbonate (Lexan)

### 3. Lightweight Competition Shells
![Lightweight competition body shell with minimalistic design](/static/images/articles/tt-02-drift-body-shell-guide-image-5.webp)

**Best For:** Competitive drifters and weight-conscious builders
- **Pros:** Reduced weight, improved handling, better power-to-weight ratio
- **Cons:** Less durable, minimal protection, higher cost
- **Features:** Thinner material, strategic reinforcement points

## DIY Body Shell Painting Guide

### Essential Tools and Materials:
- **Paint:** Polycarbonate-specific spray paints (Tamiya PS series recommended)
- **Masking:** Quality masking tape and frisket film
- **Cutting Tools:** Hobby knife, curved scissors, body reamer
- **Preparation:** Isopropyl alcohol for cleaning, lint-free cloths

### Step-by-Step Painting Process:

1.  **Body Preparation**
   - Wash body shell with soap and water
   - Clean with isopropyl alcohol to remove oils
   - Remove protective film from both sides

2.  **Masking and Design**
   - Plan your design on paper first
   - Apply masking tape for sharp lines
   - Use frisket film for complex patterns

3.  **Painting Technique**
   - Apply light coats from 6-8 inches distance
   - Start with lighter colors first
   - Allow 15-20 minutes between coats
   - Apply 3-4 coats for solid coverage

4.  **Detailing and Finishing**
   - Remove masking carefully while paint is slightly tacky
   - Apply window masks and paint interior details
   - Add decals and graphics
   - Apply clear coat for protection (optional)

### Pro Painting Tips:
- **Temperature Control:** Paint in 65-75°F (18-24°C) environment
- **Humidity:** Avoid painting in high humidity conditions
- **Ventilation:** Work in well-ventilated area or use spray booth
- **Practice:** Test techniques on scrap lexan first

## Body Shell Installation and Setup

### Mounting Hardware Requirements:
- **Body Posts:** Standard TT-02 body posts or adjustable mounts
- **Clips:** Body clips in appropriate size for your posts
- **Magnets:** Magnetic mounting systems for quick changes

### Alignment and Fitment:
- **Wheel Well Clearance:** Ensure adequate space for suspension travel
- **Body Post Height:** Adjust for proper ground clearance
- **Aerodynamic Balance:** Center body shell on chassis

### Performance Tuning with Body Shells:
- **Weight Distribution:** Balance front-to-rear weight
- **Aerodynamic Tweaks:** Add spoilers or diffusers for stability
- **Ventilation:** Cut cooling vents for electronics if needed

## Maintenance and Repair

### Cleaning and Care:
- **Regular Cleaning:** Use mild soap and water
- **Scratch Removal:** Plastic polish for minor scratches
- **Storage:** Store in protective case away from direct sunlight

### Repair Techniques:
- **Small Cracks:** Lexan cement or CA glue
- **Large Breaks:** Fiberglass reinforcement from inside
- **Paint Touch-ups:** Matching paint for small chips

## Choosing the Right Body Shell for Your TT-02

### Consider Your Driving Style:
- **Technical Drifting:** JDM styles with good downforce
- **High-Speed Drifting:** Euro or supercar styles with stability
- **Show Car:** Custom painted shells with intricate designs

### Budget Considerations:
- **Entry Level:** $20-40 for basic pre-painted shells
- **Mid Range:** $40-80 for quality pre-painted or DIY shells
- **Premium:** $80+ for competition-grade or highly detailed shells

### Skill Level Matching:
- **Beginner:** Pre-painted shells for immediate satisfaction
- **Intermediate:** Clear shells to develop painting skills
- **Advanced:** Custom designs and competition shells
